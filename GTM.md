# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy: Lumina AI Audit

## 1. The Target User Persona
The exact target user for Lumina is the **"Efficient Growth" Tech Founder** or **VPE/Engineering Manager** at a Seed to Series B startup (10–100 employees). 

At this stage, the company has likely "spammed" AI tools during their initial build phase—everyone has a Cursor Pro account, a personal ChatGPT Plus sub, and the team is testing Claude Team. The "honeymoon phase" of unlimited spending is over, and the CFO or lead investor is starting to ask about the "AI Line Item" in the burn report. These users aren't just looking for "savings"; they are looking for **benchmark validation**. They want to know if they are spending "correctly" relative to their peers. Lumina solves this by providing a defensible second opinion that they can take back to their leadership team.

## 2. Intent-Based Search & The "Moment of Need"
We target users in the exact moment they are questioning their stack. This happens during:
- **Pricing Reviews**: Googling specific comparison terms like "Cursor Business vs GitHub Copilot for teams" or "Claude Team 5 seat minimum workaround."
- **Community Venting**: Engaging in Reddit threads (`r/startups`, `r/SaaS`) or Indie Hacker posts where users complain about the rising cost of API tokens or the "death by 1,000 subscriptions."
- **Billing Spikes**: Right after a founder receives a "Your usage has exceeded 80% of your tier" email from OpenAI.

## 3. High-Density Hangouts (Distribution)
We will distribute Lumina where founders already go for "financial sanity" checks:
- **X (Twitter)**: Engaging with the "Build in Public" community. Specifically targeting threads by AI influencers who discuss "the cost of building with AI."
- **Founder Slack/Discord Groups**: Communities like *Y Combinator's Bookface*, *Launch House*, and specialized VPE groups. These are high-trust environments where a "tool that actually saves money" spreads via word-of-mouth.
- **Subreddits**: `r/startups` and `r/SaaS`. We won't just "post a link"; we will provide value by sharing an "AI Spend Report" based on our first 50 audits, then link the tool as the source.

## 4. The "Zero Dollar" First 100 Users (Tactical Plan)
1. **The "Programmatic SEO" Play on X**: Use a simple search script to find founders complaining about "AI bills" or "subscription fatigue." Reply with a personalized audit screenshot. "Hey, saw your post. I built Lumina to help benchmark this—here's what a 10-man team usually saves by consolidating. No login needed."
2. **The "Hacker News" Show & Tell**: Launch as a "Show HN." The HN crowd loves deterministic, rules-based tools that don't require an account. This is our primary engine for the "Viral Shareable Link" loop.
3. **The Investor Network**: Direct outreach to 10 Seed-stage VCs. Offer Lumina as a free "portfolio health check" they can send to their founders. It makes the VC look helpful and gives us 50+ high-quality audits in day one.

## 5. The Unfair Distribution Channel
**Credex’s Secondary Market Integration**. Credex already has a database of startups looking to *offload* excess credits. We can turn Lumina into a "Marketplace Matching" engine. When the audit identifies a $1,000/mo overspend on Claude, we don't just say "downgrade"; we say "Credex has a verified $50k credit package available right now for $35k." This transforms Lumina from a "cost calculator" into a "savings fulfillment engine."

## 6. Success Metrics: What Week 1 Traction Looks Like
If Lumina is successful, we expect to see:
- **Conversion Rate**: >40% of users who finish the audit should enter their email for the full report.
- **Viral Coefficient (K-factor)**: For every 10 users, at least 2 should share their public `/share` URL on a social platform.
- **Qualified Lead Gen**: At least 5% of users (those with >$500/mo in savings) should click the "Book a Credex Consultation" button.
- **Benchmarks**: 250 completed audits in 7 days with $0 paid spend.
